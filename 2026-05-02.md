## 🧩 3. Key Concepts  
### 🔹 Volatile vs Non‑Volatile  
- **Volatile (RAM)** loses data without power  
- **Non‑volatile (SSD/HDD/Flash)** keeps data permanently  

### 🔹 SSD vs HDD vs NVMe vs SATA vs M.2  
- **HDD** = mechanical, slow  
- **SSD** = flash, faster  
- **NVMe** = fastest, uses PCIe  
- **SATA** = older, slower interface  
- **M.2** = physical slot (can be SATA or NVMe)  

### 🔹 PCIe vs SATA  
- **PCIe** = high‑speed lanes, used by NVMe and GPUs  
- **SATA** = limited to ~600 MB/s  

### 🔹 DDR5 vs DDR4 vs DDR3  
- DDR5 adds **PMIC**, **SPD Hub**, more components  
- Higher speeds, lower voltage, better efficiency  

### 🔹 Motherboard Components  
- CPU socket  
- DDR RAM slots  
- PCIe slots  
- M.2 slots  
- Chipset  
- VRM + heatsinks  
- SATA ports  
- 24‑pin + 8‑pin power connectors  
- Rear I/O  
- CMOS battery

## 🧠 6. Troubleshooting Mindset  
- Always identify **interface vs form factor vs protocol**  
  - SATA = interface  
  - M.2 = form factor  
  - NVMe = protocol  
- Understand **power flow** on the motherboard (24‑pin → VRM → CPU/RAM)  
- Recognize that **DDR5 handles its own power regulation**  

## 📚 7. Extra Notes  
- Modern CPUs integrate the old **Northbridge** functions  
- Southbridge is now part of the **chipset**  
- PCIe generations dramatically affect NVMe performance  
- DDR5’s PMIC is a major architectural change
